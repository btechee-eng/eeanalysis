  • No complex derivations tested — just definitions, types, and formula for noise figure.

🔹 B. Must-Know Concepts
  • Thermal noise: generated by random thermal motion of electrons; power = \( kTB \)
  • Shot noise: random fluctuations in electron emission; in diodes/transistors
  • Transit-time noise: at high frequencies when transit time ≈ signal period
  • Flicker (1/f) noise: low frequency noise, power ∝ 1/f
  • White Gaussian noise: flat power spectral density across all frequencies
  • Noise temperature: equivalent temperature Te such that \( P_n = kT_eB \)
  • Noise bandwidth: effective bandwidth that produces same noise as ideal filter

🔹 B. AM Derivation (Core — All 4 Years)

Carrier: \( c(t) = A_c \cos(\omega_c t) \). Modulating signal: \( m(t) = A_m \cos(\omega_m t) \).

AM signal:

\[ s(t) = [A_c + A_m \cos(\omega_m t)] \cos(\omega_c t) \] \[ = A_c \left[1 + \frac{A_m}{A_c}\cos(\omega_m t)\right]\cos(\omega_c t) \] \[ = A_c [1 + \mu \cos(\omega_m t)]\cos(\omega_c t) \]

where modulation index: \[ \mu = \frac{A_m}{A_c} \]

Expanding:

\[ s(t) = A_c\cos(\omega_c t) + \frac{\mu A_c}{2}\cos(\omega_c + \omega_m)t + \frac{\mu A_c}{2}\cos(\omega_c - \omega_m)t \]

Modulation index from Vmax/Vmin:

\[ \mu = \frac{V_{max} - V_{min}}{V_{max} + V_{min}} \]
📌 In 2022: Vmax=10V, Vmin=5V → μ = (10-5)/(10+5) = 5/15 = 1/3 = 0.333. In 2025: same values → same answer. Learn this numerical by heart.
🔹 C. Common Mistakes
  • Forgetting to expand and show all 3 components (carrier + upper sideband + lower sideband).
  • Not deriving the Vmax/Vmin formula before substituting — always deduce it first for full marks.
  • In Balanced Modulator: forgetting that carrier is suppressed (output = DSBSC).
  • Confusing AM power: total power \( P_t = P_c(1 + \mu^2/2) \) — numerical commonly asked in Q1.

M3 Module 3 — Angle Modulation  |  HIGH Priority  |  ⏱ 6–8 hrs
🔹 A. Key Formulas

FM modulation index: \[ \beta = \frac{\Delta f}{f_m} = \frac{\text{frequency deviation}}{\text{modulating frequency}} \]

FM signal: \[ v(t) = A_c \sin(\omega_c t + \beta \sin\omega_m t) \]

Carson's Rule — transmission bandwidth:

\[ BW = 2(\Delta f + f_m) = 2f_m(1 + \beta) \]

NBFM (when β ≪ 1):

\[ v_{NBFM}(t) \approx A_c\cos\omega_c t - A_c\beta\sin\omega_m t \cdot \sin\omega_c t \]
📌 The NBFM derivation is the MOST repeated derivation — all 4 years! Master every step. Start from the FM definition, apply the approximation cos(β sinωmt) ≈ 1 and sin(β sinωmt) ≈ β sinωmt for small β.
🔹 B. FM Equation Numerical (2022 & 2025 — Same Type)

Given: \( v = 15\sin(6.5 \times 10^8 t + 5.55\sin(3 \times 1260 t)) \) volts

From this:

  • Carrier frequency: \( f_c = \frac{6.5\times10^8}{2\pi} \approx 103.4 \text{ MHz} \)
  • Modulating frequency: \( f_m = \frac{3 \times 1260}{2\pi} \approx 601.7 \text{ Hz} \)
  • Modulation index: \( \beta = 5.55 \)
  • Frequency deviation: \( \Delta f = \beta \times f_m = 5.55 \times 601.7 \approx 3339 \text{ Hz} \)
  • Power in 8Ω: \( P = \frac{A_c^2}{2R} = \frac{15^2}{2 \times 8} = \frac{225}{16} = 14.06 \text{ W} \)
⚠️ In 2025 the same equation appears with 55.55 instead of 5.55 — β = 55.55. Method is identical, only number changes.
🔹 C. FDM Bandwidth Calculation (3/4 years)

For N channels, each with bandwidth B and guard band G:

\[ BW_{total} = N \times B + (N-1) \times G \]

Example (2024): 10 channels × 4 kHz + 9 guard bands × 0.5 kHz = 40 + 4.5 = 44.5 kHz

Example (2025): 5 channels × 100 kHz + 4 guard bands × 2 kHz = 500 + 8 = 508 kHz

✓ FDM bandwidth numericals are formulaic and easy 3-mark questions. Never skip them.

M4 Module 4 — Random Signal Theory  |  MEDIUM Priority  |  ⏱ 3–4 hrs
🔹 A. Key Formulas & Definitions

CDF: \( F(x) = P(X \leq x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(u)\,du \)

PDF: \( f(x) = \frac{dF(x)}{dx} \)

Mean: \( \mu = E[X] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x\,f(x)\,dx \)

Variance: \( \sigma^2 = E[(X-\mu)^2] = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 \)

Gaussian (Normal) PDF:

\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \]

Rayleigh PDF:

\[ f(r) = \frac{r}{\sigma^2} e^{-r^2/(2\sigma^2)}, \quad r \geq 0 \]
This module appeared as a full long question only in 2022 (Q2 — 15 marks). In other years it appears only in Q1 MCQ/fill-blanks. Still prepare definitions + formulas for safety.

M5 Module 5 — Pulse Modulation  |  HIGH Priority  |  ⏱ 7–9 hrs
🔹 A. PCM SNR Formula (All 4 Years)

For a PCM system with n-bit code word and L = 2n quantization levels:

\[ \text{SNR} = \frac{3L^2}{1 + 4(L^2-1)\,P_e} \approx 3L^2 \quad \text{(for small Pe)} \]

More commonly expressed in dB:

\[ \text{SNR}_{dB} \approx 6.02n + 1.76 \text{ dB} \]

Where n = number of bits per sample. SNR improves by ~6 dB for each additional bit.

Nyquist rate: \( f_s \geq 2 f_{max} \)

For \( x(t) = 5\cos100\pi t + 10\cos200\pi t - 15\cos300\pi t \):

\[ f_{max} = \frac{300\pi}{2\pi} = 150 \text{ Hz} \implies f_s = 300 \text{ Hz} \]
📌 The Nyquist rate for this exact signal appeared in 2022 Q5(b), 2024 Q1(v), and 2025 Q6(c). Memorize the answer: 300 Hz, Nyquist interval = 1/300 s.
🔹 B. TDM Bit Rate Numerical (2025 — 7 marks)

Given: 24 channels, 8000 samples/sec each, 8 bits/sample, 1 sync bit per frame.

Total bits per frame = \( 24 \times 8 + 1 = 193 \) bits

Total bit rate = \( 193 \times 8000 = 1{,}544{,}000 \text{ bits/sec} = 1.544 \text{ Mbps} \)

✓ This is exactly T1 carrier — a standard telecom result. Memorize the 1.544 Mbps figure.
🔹 C. PCM Numerical Examples

2023 Q5(b): TV signal, 1024 quantization levels.

  • Code word length: \( n = \log_2(1024) = 10 \) bits
  • Output SNR ≈ \( 6.02 \times 10 + 1.76 = 61.96 \text{ dB} \)

2024 Q5(b): Analog signal fmax = 40 kHz, 16 quantization levels.

  • n = log₂(16) = 4 bits
  • Nyquist rate = 2 × 40 = 80 ksamples/sec
  • Bit rate = 80,000 × 4 = 320 kbps

2025: Sinusoidal signal amplitude 12 V, 16 level quantizer:

  • Step size = \( \frac{2 \times 12}{16} = \frac{24}{16} = 1.5 \text{ V} \)

🔹 A. Key Concepts
FSK generation + coherent detection appeared in 4/4 years as a major question. This is the most repeated combined question in Module 6. Master both generation circuit AND coherent detection block diagram.

M2 Amplitude Modulation  |  HIGH — Study First  |  8 Lectures
🔴 Highest impact module. AM derivation + modulation index formula appeared ALL 4 years. Balanced Modulator appeared 3/4 years. Together these two topics account for ~12–15 marks every year. Master them completely.
TopicFreqStatusWhat to Study / What to Skip
AM signal expression derivation (single tone) 4/4 ✅ MUST Derive from scratch: s(t)=Ac[1+μcos(ωmt)]cos(ωct). Show expansion into carrier + USB + LSB. Memorize every step. Questions: 2022 Q3(a), 2023 Q2(a), 2024 Q2(b), 2025 Q2(a).
Modulation index from Vmax/Vmin 3/4 ✅ MUST Formula: \(\mu = (V_{max}-V_{min})/(V_{max}+V_{min})\). Deduce it from the AM expression before substituting. Numerical: Vmax=10, Vmin=5 → μ=1/3. Appeared 2022, 2025.
Balanced Modulator working principle 3/4 ✅ MUST Draw circuit with 2 AM modulators, opposite carrier polarity. Carriers cancel → DSBSC output. Explain mathematically: output = 2Am cosωmt × Ac cosωct. Questions: 2023–2025.
Square Law Modulator (short note) 3/4 ✅ MUST Device output: i=a₁v+a₂v². Show how v² term produces AM signal. Include BPF to select desired component. Appeared 2023, 2024, 2025 Q7.
Why modulation is needed 2/4 STUDY 4 reasons: practical antenna size (λ/4), multiplexing, noise immunity, long-distance transmission. One paragraph, 4 bullet points. Worth 3–4 marks when asked.
AM total power formula + numericals 2/4 STUDY Formula: Pt=Pc(1+μ²/2). Appears in Q1 fill-blanks (1–2 marks). E.g., Pc=800W, μ=0.5 → Pt=900W. Learn formula — 1 minute to solve.
Envelope Detector 1/4 STUDY Diode + RC circuit. Output follows envelope. RC condition: 1/fc ≪ RC ≪ 1/fm. Appeared as short note 2025 Q7(c). Simple diagram required.
DSBSC vs SSB explanation 0/4 SKIM Know definitions and bandwidth only (DSBSC: BW=2fm, carrier suppressed; SSB: BW=fm, only one sideband). No full question asked. May appear in Q1 fill-blanks.
Power spectra of AM / DSBSC / SSB derivation 0/4 SKIP Never asked as a standalone question. Know bandwidth formulas only. Skip full power spectrum derivation.
Switching modulator / ring modulator details 0/4 SKIP Mentioned in syllabus but never appeared in PYQ. Safe to skip circuit-level details.

M3 Angle Modulation  |  HIGH — Study Second  |  8 Lectures
🔴 NBFM derivation is the single most repeated derivation in this entire subject — all 4 years, varying marks from 6–10. Armstrong Modulator appeared all 4 years (as short note or long answer). FDM bandwidth numerical appeared all 4 years. These 3 topics alone = 15–20 marks.
TopicFreqStatusWhat to Study / What to Skip
NBFM derivation (full derivation) 4/4 ✅ MUST Start: v(t)=Ac cos[ωct+β sinωmt]. Apply small angle: cos(β sinωmt)≈1, sin(β sinωmt)≈β sinωmt. Result: vNBFM = Ac cosωct − Acβ sinωmt·sinωct. Write every algebra step.
Armstrong (Indirect) FM Modulator 4/4 ✅ MUST Block diagram: Crystal oscillator → Phase modulator (NBFM) → Frequency multiplier → Mixer/Up-converter → WBFM output. Explain each stage role. 5-mark short note or 5-mark long answer subpart.
FDM bandwidth numerical 4/4 ✅ MUST Formula: BW = N×channel_BW + (N−1)×guard_band. Practice all 3 past numericals: 2023 (509 kHz), 2024 (44.5 kHz), 2025 (508 kHz). Very easy 3–5 mark question.
Carson's Rule for FM bandwidth 3/4 ✅ MUST BW = 2(Δf+fm) = 2fm(1+β). Numerical: Δf=15kHz, fm=3kHz → BW=36kHz. Appeared in 2022, 2023, 2025 Q1. 1–3 marks, solve in 2 minutes.
FM signal equation numericals (5 unknowns) 2/4 ✅ MUST Given v=Ac sin(ωct+β sinωmt): find fc, fm, β, Δf, power. Appeared 2022 Q4(b) and 2025 Q3(a) — almost identical. β=coefficient of inner sinusoid, fc=outer angular coefficient/2π, P=Ac²/2R.
WBFM vs NBFM differences 1/4 STUDY Table: NBFM β<0.3, BW≈2fm, low distortion; WBFM β>1, BW=2(Δf+fm), wideband. Appeared 2024 Q3(a) as 4-mark subpart. Prepare comparison table.
FM signal deviation proportional to amplitude 1/4 STUDY Δf ∝ Am. If voltage changes from V1→V2, Δf2=Δf1×(V2/V1). Easy 3-mark numerical (2024 Q3(b)). One formula, one substitution.
PLL demodulation for FM 0/4 SKIM Never a standalone question. Know: PLL tracks FM carrier, VCO control voltage = demodulated signal. 2 lines of definition only.
Super heterodyne receiver 1/4 SKIM Block diagram only: RF amp → Mixer (LO) → IF amp → Detector → Audio. IF is fixed (455kHz AM, 10.7MHz FM). Appeared once 2023 Q7(f). Low probability — 10 min preparation.
Frequency discrimination demodulator (full circuit) 0/4 SKIP Never asked in detail. Know the name only — frequency discriminator converts FM to AM then demodulates.
Direct FM generation (reactance modulator) 0/4 SKIP Syllabus mentions "direct method" but never tested. Skip circuit details. Armstrong (indirect) is all you need for FM generation questions.
⏱ Time budget: 6–7 hours. Day 2: NBFM derivation (3h — practice 3 times). Day 3: Armstrong + FDM numericals (2.5h). Day 11: Revision write-from-memory (1h).
M5 Pulse Modulation  |  HIGH — Study Third  |  8 Lectures
🔴 Highest mark-yielding module. PCM SNR appeared all 4 years (6–10 marks each time). TDM appeared all 4 years. Nyquist rate numerical appeared all 4 years. Delta Modulator appeared all 4 years as a short note. This module can yield 25–30 marks across Q1, long questions, and Q7.
TopicFreqStatusWhat to Study / What to Skip
PCM SNR derivation 4/4 ✅ MUST Key steps: Quantization noise power = Δ²/12. Signal power (sinusoidal) = A²/2. L = 2ⁿ levels. SNR = 3L². In dB: SNR ≈ 6.02n + 1.76. Each bit adds ~6 dB. Write the full derivation — 6–10 marks.
Nyquist rate numericals 4/4 ✅ MUST For x(t) = 5cos100πt + 10cos200πt − 15cos300πt: highest freq = 150 Hz, Nyquist = 300 Hz, interval = 1/300 s. This exact signal appeared 2022, 2024, 2025. Memorize the answer.
TDM concept + bit rate numerical 4/4 ✅ MUST TDM: time slots per channel. Bit rate = channels × bits_per_sample × sampling_rate + sync_bits. 2025 numerical: 24×8+1=193 bits/frame × 8000 = 1.544 Mbps (T1). Learn T1 result.
Types of pulse modulation (PAM/PWM/PPM) 4/4 ✅ MUST PAM: amplitude varies. PWM: width varies with amplitude. PPM: position varies. Draw all 3 waveforms for same input signal. Appeared every year 3–6 marks. Also explain generation: comparator + sawtooth for PWM.
Delta Modulator (short note) 4/4 ✅ MUST Block diagram: comparator + integrator + 1-bit quantizer feedback. Waveform showing step-wise tracking. Slope overload: input changes too fast. Granular noise: step size too large. 5 marks every year in Q7.
Compander (short note) 3/4 ✅ MUST Companding = Compression + Expansion. μ-law: V_out = Vmax × ln(1+μ|Vin|/Vmax)/ln(1+μ). Block diagram: compressor → channel → expander. Improves SNR for weak signals. 5 marks in Q7.
Sample and Hold circuit 2/4 STUDY FET switch + capacitor + buffer. Sample phase: switch ON, capacitor charges to signal level. Hold: switch OFF, capacitor holds value. Draw circuit and waveform. Appeared 2022, 2023.
PCM concept + generation block diagram 2/4 STUDY Block: Sampler → Quantizer → Encoder → PCM bits. Reconstruction: Decoder → DAC → LPF. 2–3 marks when asked as subpart (2023, 2024). One paragraph explanation.
PCM numerical (code word length, bit rate) 2/4 STUDY n = log₂L. Bit rate = n × fs. Step size = (Vmax−Vmin)/L. Solve: 2023 (L=1024→n=10, SNR=61.96 dB), 2024 (fmax=40kHz, L=16→320 kbps), 2025 (A=12V, L=16→step=1.5V).
Quantizer types (uniform/non-uniform) 2/4 STUDY Uniform: equal step size Δ=(Vmax−Vmin)/L. Non-uniform (μ-law/A-law): smaller steps near zero → better SNR for weak signals. Midrise vs midtread. Appeared 2024 Q7(e), 2025 Q7(d).
FDM concept + bandwidth 2/4 STUDY FDM: each channel occupies different frequency band. BW = N×channel_BW + (N−1)×guard_band. Also studied under M3 but PCM questions sometimes include FDM subpart.
Signaling formats for PCM 0/4 SKIM Know NRZ, RZ, AMI names only. Never a standalone question. 2 minutes to read — may appear in 1-mark fill-blank.
Adaptive delta modulation 0/4 SKIP In syllabus but never tested. Know the name: step size varies to reduce slope overload. Skip circuit details.
⏱ Time budget: 7–9 hours. Days 4–6: PAM/PWM/PPM + S&H + PCM SNR + TDM + Delta + Compander. Day 12: Numericals revision batch. PCM SNR alone needs 2–3 hours of derivation practice.
