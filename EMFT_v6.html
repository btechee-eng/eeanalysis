📚 Section 3 — Module-wise Study Strategy

M1 Module 1 — Vector Analysis  HIGH  3 lectures
🎯 This module appears in Q1 (MCQ/short) AND as full derivation questions. Every year has coordinate transformation and nature-of-field checks. High marks for low effort if formulas are memorised.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

4/4 years Transform a vector field from Cartesian to cylindrical coordinates (e.g. A = xyza_x + x²yza_y + xyz²a_z)
M1Easy-Med 4–5 marks
4/4 years Find nature of vector field F — is it solenoidal (∇·F=0) and/or irrotational (∇×F=0)?
M1Easy 4–6 marks
3/4 years Verify Divergence Theorem for a given vector over a volume (e.g. A = (10ρ³/4)a_φ, ρ=2, z=0 to 10)
M1Medium 5–6 marks

Key Formulas to Memorise:

Cartesian to Cylindrical: \(\rho = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\), \(\phi = \tan^{-1}(y/x)\), \(z=z\)
Cartesian to Spherical: \(r = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\), \(\theta = \cos^{-1}(z/r)\), \(\phi = \tan^{-1}(y/x)\)

Divergence in cylindrical: \[\nabla \cdot \mathbf{A} = \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial(\rho A_\rho)}{\partial \rho} + \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\partial A_\phi}{\partial \phi} + \frac{\partial A_z}{\partial z}\]

Curl in cylindrical: \[\nabla \times \mathbf{A} = \frac{1}{\rho}\begin{vmatrix} \hat{a}_\rho & \rho\hat{a}_\phi & \hat{a}_z \\ \partial/\partial\rho & \partial/\partial\phi & \partial/\partial z \\ A_\rho & \rho A_\phi & A_z \end{vmatrix}\]

Common Mistakes:

⚠️ Not substituting x=ρcosφ, y=ρsinφ in vector components before transforming. Always transform BOTH position and unit vectors.
⚠️ Forgetting the ρ in the Jacobian when converting volume elements: dV = ρ dρ dφ dz

Topics to Skim (rarely asked):

  • General curvilinear coordinates derivation — never appeared directly
  • Metric coefficients in general coordinates
M2 Module 2 — Static Electric Field  HIGH  9 lectures
🎯 Largest module. Energy density + Gauss's Law appear EVERY year. These 2 topics alone can fetch 10–12 marks. Master both derivations completely.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

4/4 years Derive expression for energy stored in electrostatic field for an assembly of charges. Also find energy density \(w_E = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{D}\cdot\mathbf{E}\)
M2Medium 5–7 marks
3/4 years Applying Gauss's Law, find electric field intensity E at a point due to an infinitely long line charge with charge density ρ_L C/m
M2Medium 5–6 marks
3/4 years Derive expression for boundary conditions at a dielectric–dielectric interface — tangential and normal components of E and D
M2Medium 4–6 marks

Key Formulas:

Gauss's Law (integral form): \[\oint_S \mathbf{D} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = Q_{enc}\]

E due to infinite line charge: \[\mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho_L}{2\pi\varepsilon_0 \rho}\hat{a}_\rho \quad \text{V/m}\]

Energy density in electric field: \[w_E = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon E^2 = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{D}\cdot\mathbf{E} \quad \text{J/m}^3\]

Potential due to point charge: \[V = \frac{Q}{4\pi\varepsilon_0 r} \quad \text{V}\]

Boundary condition — tangential E: \[E_{t1} = E_{t2}\]

Boundary condition — normal D: \[D_{n1} - D_{n2} = \rho_s\]

Common Mistakes:

⚠️ In energy derivation: don't forget the ½ factor that comes from the work done to build up the charge system step by step.
⚠️ In Gauss's Law: choosing wrong Gaussian surface (use cylinder for line charge, sphere for point charge).
M3 Module 3 — Static Magnetic Field  HIGH  7 lectures
🎯 Magnetic scalar/vector potential and Biot-Savart Law are asked EVERY year. Modified Ampere's Law appears ALL 4 years. These 3 topics = guaranteed 15+ marks.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

4/4 years Write short notes on magnetic scalar potential and vector magnetic potential. Given \(\mathbf{A} = -(\rho^2/4)\hat{a}_z\) Wb/m, calculate total magnetic flux crossing a surface.
M3Medium 5 marks
3/4 years Applying Biot-Savart Law, derive magnetic field intensity H at point P due to an infinite straight current conductor.
M3Medium 5–6 marks
3/4 years Find current density J given magnetic field H = sin(x) a_y. Also find H at centre of square current loop.
M3Medium 5 marks

Key Formulas:

Biot-Savart Law: \[d\mathbf{H} = \frac{I\,d\mathbf{l} \times \hat{a}_R}{4\pi R^2} \quad \text{A/m}\]

H due to infinite straight conductor: \[\mathbf{H} = \frac{I}{2\pi \rho}\hat{a}_\phi \quad \text{A/m}\]

Ampere's Law (integral): \[\oint_L \mathbf{H} \cdot d\mathbf{l} = I_{enc}\]

Vector magnetic potential B: \[\mathbf{B} = \nabla \times \mathbf{A}\]

Magnetic flux: \[\Psi = \int_S \mathbf{B} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = \int_S (\nabla\times\mathbf{A})\cdot d\mathbf{S}\]

Maxwell's divergence: \[\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0\]

Common Mistakes:

⚠️ In Biot-Savart: forgetting sin(θ) in the cross product, and limits of integration for finite vs infinite conductor.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

4/4 years Explain significance of displacement current. Show how it led to modification of Ampere's Law (Maxwell's 2nd curl equation). \[\nabla \times \mathbf{H} = \mathbf{J} + \frac{\partial\mathbf{D}}{\partial t}\]
M4Medium 5–7 marks
4/4 years State Poynting theorem. Derive expression for Poynting vector. Calculate average power per unit area for sinusoidal wave in a medium.
M4Medium 5–9 marks
3/4 years Loss tangent significance and numerical: given σ, ε_r, μ_r and frequency, find loss tangent \(\tan\delta = \sigma/\omega\varepsilon\). Also find ratio of conduction to displacement current.
M4Easy 4–5 marks
2/4 years List all Maxwell's equations in both integral and differential (point) form for steady and time-varying fields.
M4Easy 4–6 marks

Maxwell's Equations — Must Memorise:

Faraday's Law: \[\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}\]

Ampere-Maxwell: \[\nabla \times \mathbf{H} = \mathbf{J} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{D}}{\partial t}\]

Gauss's Electric: \[\nabla \cdot \mathbf{D} = \rho_v\]

Gauss's Magnetic: \[\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0\]

Poynting Vector: \[\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{E} \times \mathbf{H} \quad \text{W/m}^2\]

Average Power: \[P_{avg} = \frac{1}{2}\text{Re}(\mathbf{E}\times\mathbf{H}^*)\]

Displacement Current Numerical Pattern (2023 PYQ):

2023 Parallel plates: area = \(4\times10^{-4}\) m², separation = 0.4 cm, V = 10 sin(10³t) V, ε_r = μ. Find displacement current.

Solution approach: \(J_d = \varepsilon\frac{\partial E}{\partial t} = \varepsilon\frac{\partial (V/d)}{\partial t}\)

M4Easy 5 marks · 2023

Common Mistakes:

⚠️ In Poynting theorem derivation: forgetting to use vector identity \(\nabla\cdot(\mathbf{E}\times\mathbf{H})\) as the starting point.

M6 Module 6 — Poisson's & Laplace's Equations  HIGH  3 lectures
🎯 Short module but high yield. Method of images, Laplace solution (cylindrical/spherical), and uniqueness theorem are standard exam questions — relatively easy to prepare with formulas.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

2/4 years Evaluate potential, E-field and surface charge density due to a point charge above a grounded conducting plane using method of images.
M6Medium 6 marks · 2022
1/4 years Apply Laplace's equation to find potential between a spherical capacitor of radius a and b. BCs: V = V₀ at r = a, V = 0 at r = b.
M6Medium 5 marks · 2025
2/4 years Prove uniqueness theorem for Poisson's and Laplace's equation.
M6Medium 5–6 marks · 2022, 2024

Key Equations:

Poisson's equation: \[\nabla^2 V = -\frac{\rho_v}{\varepsilon}\]

Laplace's equation (free space): \[\nabla^2 V = 0\]

Laplace in spherical (radial only): \[\frac{1}{r^2}\frac{d}{dr}\left(r^2\frac{dV}{dr}\right) = 0 \implies V = \frac{A}{r} + B\]

Laplace in cylindrical (radial only): \[\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{d}{d\rho}\left(\rho\frac{dV}{d\rho}\right) = 0 \implies V = A\ln\rho + B\]

M7 Module 7 — EM Waves  HIGH  8 lectures
🎯 Highest priority module — appears in nearly every long question. Wave equation derivation, skin depth numericals, reflection/transmission ALL appear every year. Prepare ALL sub-topics.

Top topics by PYQ frequency:

4/4 years Derive reflection and transmission coefficients (Γ and τ) for uniform plane wave at normal incidence on a perfect dielectric. Intrinsic impedance form.
M7Medium 4–7 marks
4/4 years Starting with Maxwell's equations, derive wave equation for E in (a) perfect dielectric (b) lossy medium / good conductor.
M7Hard 6–7 marks
3/4 years Find skin depth δ, propagation constant γ, and wave velocity v at frequency 1.6 MHz in aluminium (σ = 38.2 MS/m, μ_r = 1).
M7Easy 4–5 marks
3/4 years Transmission line: given v_p = 2.5×10⁸ m/s, C = 30 pF/m, find L, β at 100 MHz, and characteristic impedance Z₀.
M7Easy 5–6 marks · 2023, 2025

Key Wave Propagation Formulas:

General propagation constant: \[\gamma = \alpha + j\beta = j\omega\sqrt{\mu\varepsilon}\sqrt{1 - j\frac{\sigma}{\omega\varepsilon}}\]

Good conductor (σ ≫ ωε): \[\alpha = \beta = \sqrt{\frac{\omega\mu\sigma}{2}}\]

Skin depth: \[\delta = \frac{1}{\alpha} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\omega\mu\sigma}}\]

Intrinsic impedance of medium: \[\eta = \sqrt{\frac{j\omega\mu}{\sigma + j\omega\varepsilon}}\]

Reflection coefficient: \[\Gamma = \frac{\eta_2 - \eta_1}{\eta_2 + \eta_1}\]

Transmission coefficient: \[\tau = \frac{2\eta_2}{\eta_2 + \eta_1}\]

Characteristic impedance (lossless line): \[Z_0 = \sqrt{\frac{L}{C}}\]

Phase constant (lossless): \[\beta = \omega\sqrt{LC}\]

Skin Depth Numerical — Repeated 3 Times (2022, 2023, 2025):

✅ For aluminium: σ = 38.2 × 10⁶ S/m, μ_r = 1, f = 1.6 MHz
δ = 1/√(πfμσ) = 64.5 μm approx. This exact numerical appears in 3 years — memorise the approach!

Common Mistakes:

⚠️ In wave equation derivation: must take curl of Maxwell's curl equation twice and use vector identity ∇×(∇×E) = ∇(∇·E) − ∇²E, then use ∇·E = 0 for source-free region.
⚠️ Transmission line: don't confuse distortionless (R/L = G/C) with lossless (R=G=0).
